About: History of Bodybuilding

This page is meant to cover the general history of bodybuilding, starting in the Bronze era, and leading into the modern day era. Each section will give a brief overview of each era and the significant details in each.

Bronze Era

There isn't a lot of information on this era, due to the lack of gyms and different equipment being used, with the time period having a larger focus on strongman competitions versus bodybuilding. Eugen Sandow is known as the 'Father of Bodybuilding' due to incoporating posing into his strongman routines, which directed focus from the lifts and more toward the lifter. A lot of things prevalant in bodybuilding today didn't exist back then as well, such as dieting and performance enhancing drugs (PEDs).

Silver Era

Bodybuilding saw a larger surge in popularity during this era, due to the physique of John Grimek. Popularity began to rise as gyms became more common, and bodybuilding competitions started to become more popular. Dieting didn't have much advancement, but the general bodybuilding lifestyle started to become more relevant as magazines provided information on diet and exercise. PEDs were first created in this era, with the invention of Dianabol in the late 50s.

Golden Era

Bodybuilding became much more mainstream in this era, with the rise of Arnold Schwarzenegger in the bodybuilding scene. PEDs became regularly available and used in the scene, with them being used in most competitions for bodybuilders to stay relevant in the first place. Physiques were very aesthetic in this era, and this era provided some of the most desired physiques by thte general public. Dieting became much more focused on low carbohydrates and high protein.

Mass Monster Era

The Mass Monster Era started to rise with Lee Haney, where he dominated the bodybuilding scene for a large period of time. His physique wasn't absurdly large, but still pushed the limits of what was considered 'aesthetic' during the Golden Era. Judges started to favor more size over symmetry. Dorian Yates winning the Olympia ushered in the Mass Monster era, where he would win the competitoin while getting larger and larger every year. The era is defined by the amount of muscle a bodybuilder has, versus their symmetry or aesthetics. PEDs are extremely prevalant in this era and the modern day, with the reprocussions pretty well known, but still used regardless. There is a demand in the modern day for a return to the more aesthetic physiques of the Golden Era.

More Information

When training, I will usually pick one compound movement and one isolaton movement per muscle group. For example, when traning the quads, I'll do a squat variation and a leg extension.

I usually aim to hit 6-8 working sets per muscle group.

Exercises:

  • Legs: Squat Variation (Hack Squat, Pendulum Squat, Barbell Squat), Romanian Deadlift, Leg Extension, Leg Curl, Calf Raises
  • Chest: Bench Press Variation (Incline Bench, Incline Smith Machine Bench, Barbell Bench), Chest Fly (Cable, Machine), Machine Press
  • Back: Barbell Row or Deadlift, Lat Pulldown, Pullover, Lat Isolation (Kneeling Lat Pulldown, Machine Row)
  • Arms: Hammer Curls, Ez-Bar Curls, or some other bicep curl, Tricep Pushdown, Lateral Raises, Reverse Curls

Programming

Programming provides a structure for someone to follow week-by-week to provide consistent progress, which can be helpful for both beginner and advanced lifters. The main site that I use to find programs is Lift Vault, where I personally found programs when I started out and when I was preparing for my powerlifting meet. Personally, I don't really use a program right now since I'm planning my own hypertrophy, but they help for someone who needs structure to start off with.

Coaching

Having a coach or personal trainer can be very beneficial to lifters at any stage. Having someone who's studied and been certified in lifting can help you with basic form, technique, and knowing what to do when you go into the gym. Even besides a coach, haivng someone that can act as a mentor to guide you through the beginning stages of lifting can allow you to make fantastic process early on and even further into your lifting journey.

Why Bodybuilding?
